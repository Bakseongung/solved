If the square root of 2 is a rational number,
it can be expressed as a fraction in terms of two integers p and q. 
In this case,
2 = p²/q² → p² = 2q², so p and q are even numbers.
That is, they have a common factor of 2.
However, there are situations where we can still divide the fraction
even though we used numbers that are mathematically coprime—meaning they cannot be further divided.
Since this is a contradiction, we must accept the existence of irrational numbers.
